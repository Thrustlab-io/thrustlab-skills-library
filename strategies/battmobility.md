#### **Tone & Voice Guidelines**

**Do:**
- ‚úÖ Be direct and specific ("Save ‚Ç¨120K" not "save money")
- ‚úÖ Use data and numbers (builds CFO credibility)
- ‚úÖ Lead with customer outcomes, not features
- ‚úÖ Acknowledge uncertainty where it exists (builds trust)
- ‚úÖ Use Belgian context (2026 tax law, local geography, language)
- ‚úÖ Confident but not arrogant ("This works" vs. "We're the best")

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Use jargon or buzzwords ("synergy", "revolutionary", "disruptive")
- ‚ùå Overpromise ("Slash costs by 80%!" - not credible)
- ‚ùå Sell features ("We have an app!") vs. outcomes
- ‚ùå Sound desperate ("Can I get 5 minutes?" - no value exchange)
- ‚ùå Be vague ("significant savings", "improved efficiency" - quantify!)
- ‚ùå Ignore objections or be defensive

**Language:**
- Professional but conversational (not stiff corporate-speak)
- Bilingual context: English for international companies, Dutch/French for local
- Short sentences, simple words (respect recipient's time)

#### **Cadence Management Best Practices**

**1. Multi-Channel Orchestration:**
- Email = primary (work inbox, measurable)
- LinkedIn = secondary (builds relationship, less formal)
- Never send email + LinkedIn same day (feels spammy)
- Space touches 2-3 days apart minimum

**2. Subject Line Strategy:**
- First email: Curiosity + specificity (`{{company_name}} - 2026 fleet tax exposure`)
- Follow-ups: Use `Re:` to thread (feels like continuation, not spam)
- Avoid: "Touching base", "Following up", "Checking in" (low value)

**3. CTA Ladder (Decreasing Commitment):**
- Touch 1: "15-minute call" (standard ask)
- Touch 2: "Quick conversation" (softer)
- Touch 3: "Either/or question" (easier to reply)
- Touch 4: "I'll send you X" (no ask, pure value) + referral request

**4. Value Escalation:**
- Touch 1: Identify pain + high-level solution
- Touch 2: Social proof (case study, numbers)
- Touch 3: New angle or insight (keep it fresh)
- Touch 4: High-value resource + graceful exit

**5. Personalization Tokens by Tier:**
- **Tier 1 (High-Touch):** Custom first line, specific company trigger, named reference
- **Tier 2 (Medium-Touch):** Template + 1-2 custom details (hiring, location, size)
- **Tier 3 (Low-Touch):** Auto-populated variables only (`{{company_name}}`, `{{industry}}`)

**6. Exit Strategy:**
- Touch 4 = graceful exit ("last note from me")
- Offer value even if no reply (playbook, newsletter opt-in)
- Referral request (turn "no" into potential warm intro)
- Move to long-term nurture (quarterly check-ins, not weekly)

**7. Response Handling:**
- **Positive reply:** Immediately pause sequence, send calendar link, qualify
- **"Not now" reply:** Pause sequence, ask timeline, move to nurture list
- **"Unsubscribe" / "Stop":** Immediately remove, honor request, update CRM
- **Out-of-office:** Pause sequence, resume 1 week after return date

## RISK MITIGATION

**What Could Go Wrong:**

**Risk 1: Low Reply Rates (<8%)**
- **Cause:** Poor targeting (ICP mismatch), weak messaging, bad timing
- **Mitigation:** A/B test aggressively, tighten ICP filters, move to Tier 1 (high-touch)

**Risk 2: High Bounce Rates (>5%)**
- **Cause:** Bad email data, unverified contacts
- **Mitigation:** Use NeverBounce, improve data sources, slow send ramp

**Risk 3: Spam Complaints**
- **Cause:** Irrelevant messaging, too aggressive cadence
- **Mitigation:** Better targeting, softer CTAs, respect "not now" replies

**Risk 4: Tool Integration Failures**
- **Cause:** API limits, incorrect field mapping
- **Mitigation:** Test integrations with small batches, have backup workflows

**Risk 5: Sales Team Overwhelmed**
- **Cause:** Too many meetings booked, not enough AE capacity
- **Mitigation:** Control outbound volume, qualify harder, hire SDR #2

- **Allowed:** B2B outbound to work emails (legitimate interest)
- **Required:** Unsubscribe link in every email, honor opt-outs within 24 hours
- **Prohibited:** Scraping personal emails (Gmail, Hotmail), continuing after "unsubscribe"
- **Best Practice:** Document data sources, provide privacy policy link

- **Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
- **Requirement:** Mandatory structured e-invoicing for domestic B2B transactions
- **Impact:** BattMobility must implement e-invoicing for corporate clients
